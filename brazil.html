Lençóis Maranhenses National Park, Brazil

The most surreal swimming experience you will ever have will be on this place.




Jump to Bottom


About the Place: The Lençóis Maranhenses National Park in Brazil features vast, rolling sand dunes that stretch as far as the eye can see, interspersed with crystal-clear freshwater lagoons that form during the rainy season. This unique landscape creates a striking contrast of white sand and turquoise water, making it look almost otherworldly. The dunes are shaped constantly by the wind, creating ever-changing patterns and a serene, expansive environment. Visitors often explore the park on guided tours, walking or driving across the dunes and swimming in the lagoons.

Why I Want To Visit: This is a white desert and has lakes wherein you can swim on. Tell me that's not part of your life goal, because it is for me. I want to come back to the Philippines and be able to tell my relatives that I have swam somewhere very cool such as this sand dune, and they would not believe it!

Fun Fact: During the rainy season, some lagoons between the dunes can reach up to two meters deep, forming temporary freshwater pools that support local wildlife and offer perfect spots for swimming amid the desert-like scenery.
