Waitomo Glowworm Caves, New Zealand

This is a cave that made itself like the stars in the sky.




Jump to Bottom


About the Place: The Waitomo Glowworm Caves in New Zealand are famous for their ceilings that shimmer with thousands of bioluminescent glowworms, creating the illusion of a star-filled night sky underground. Formed over millions of years by limestone and water erosion, the caves feature dramatic stalactites, stalagmites, and underground rivers. Visitors often glide silently through the dark caverns by boat, enhancing the magical, otherworldly atmosphere. The glowworms’ soft blue-green light is produced by a natural chemical reaction within their bodies.

Why I Want To Visit: The glowworm caves look like stars. Personally, I have not seen anything bioluminescent aside from fireflies and I would love to see them occur naturally from terrains. Coursing through the river whilst looking upward to see the glowing creatures must be very dreamy.

Fun Fact: The glowworms in these caves are not actually worms at all—they’re the larval stage of a fungus gnat species found only in New Zealand, meaning the dazzling display you see is essentially thousands of tiny insects lighting up the cave to attract prey.
