Salar de Uyuni, Bolivia

This would be Earth's largest mirror.





Jump to Bottom


About the Place: The Salar de Uyuni in Bolivia is the world’s largest salt flat, stretching over 10,000 square kilometers. After rains, the flat transforms into a giant mirror, perfectly reflecting the sky and creating a surreal, endless horizon. Its vast white expanse is made of thick salt crusts that sparkle under the sun, and beneath lies a rich reserve of lithium. Tourists often visit for stunning photography, especially during sunrise and sunset, when the flat’s reflective surface produces otherworldly colors.

Why I Want To Visit:The salt flats look like a portion of Heaven on Earth. It looks like a place that appears in your dreams, and imagine how surreal it is seeing it with your own eyes. Driving a vehicle must be fun here because the fields look vast and boundless.

Fun Fact: Salar de Uyuni is so flat that it’s used to calibrate satellites, making it one of the most important natural landmarks for space technology in the world.
