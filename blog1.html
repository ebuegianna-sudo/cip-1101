The Uncomfortable Truth About Programming

Written By: Penny Wrinkle

Date: 02/03/2026





Practicality Over Passion?

People view programming in many ways. To some, it is a niche hobby. To others, it is a tool used to earn money aside from solving problems. However, programming is not just about building softwares. And being a programmer yourself is not just about being able to code. Doing it is difficult and many overlook the reason why before choosing that path because it is admittedly one of the most practical things you can do in today’s society having technology as one of its biggest backbones. This blog will uncover the layers of what it truly takes to be a real programmer.



Coding vs Typing

Programmer 1 codes to learn, and Programmer 2 codes to write code, however both know how to code. Truly, there are a lot of code templates online in which people can paste anytime and anywhere. This does not automatically diminish learning. Let college be our scenery here. I have observed that this method is very rampant especially to those with beginner-level knowledge, and on top of that, they have to adjust themselves to strict time constraints school is setting for them. With a setting like this, copying and pasting can be resourceful at first, but when that becomes a habit, learners might not realize that they are slowly carving a path that leads to nothing. Requirements may have been passed but there was no real learning there. Real programming takes time. It requires showing up frequently, exploring more, trying different things, solving small problems at times, and testing yourself about what you have learned. It is about self-diagnosis, asking yourself what your knowledge gaps are and constantly catching up. It is about knowing what it’s like to sit through one problem for five hours, all sweaty, and mentally hitting a brick wall. Ultimately, it is about resilience, and accepting the fact that you will fail over and over again. When all these things come to you at once, it is important to think about what you can learn now and not criticize yourself for not being the professional programmer you want to be yet. That’s just unrealistic. However, I myself fall victim to this sometimes hence I try to remind myself just how broad of a field programming is. It is hard at first but that is the point. The point is to learn enough to make what was once difficult easy, and to shape yourself to be the kind of programmer that’s courageous to learn pretty much anything.



What Mastery Really Means

Programming requires real commitment. You don’t have to solve every arithmetic problem in the world successfully, nor does your progress have to be speedy. It is about showing up and developing your passion and willingness to learn it despite knowing that it is not the easiest thing to do. Programming may be technical, but it also involves heart in such a way that you have cared enough to be knowledgeable about it, and that you have become the type of programmer that is able to help everyone through the means of technology. Whether you are a student or an independent learner from home, programming is possible and is a challenge that can be overcome as long as real work is present.
