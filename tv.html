K-Drama

Hotel Del Luna

Sub-genre: Dark Fantasy, Romantic Comedy, Supernatural.

Director: Oh Choong-hwan

Starring: Lee Ji-eun (IU), Yeo Jin-goo


Synopsis: Jang Man-wol is the moody owner of a mysterious hotel that only caters to ghosts with unfinished business before they move to the afterlife. She is forced to hire a human manager, Gu Chan-sung, who slowly unravels the tragic secrets of her 1,300-year-old past.


Goblin (Guardian: The Lonely and Great God)

Sub-genre: Fantasy, Romance, Melodrama.

Director: Lee Eung-bok

Starring: Gong Yoo, Kim Go-eun, Lee Dong-wook, Yoo In-na



Synopsis: Kim Shin is an immortal goblin who has waited 900 years for a human bride to pull the sword from his chest and end his cursed life. His destiny becomes intertwined with a high school student who can see ghosts and a grim reaper who has no memory of his past.


Business Proposal

Sub-genre: Romantic Comedy, Office Drama.

Director: Park Seon-ho

Starring: Ahn Hyo-seop, Kim Se-jeong, Kim Min-kyu, Seol In-ah


Synopsis: Shin Ha-ri goes on a blind date in place of her wealthy friend to get rejected, only to discover that her date is actually the CEO of her company. Things spiral out of control when he decides to marry her to stop his grandfather from sending him on more dates, forcing her to lead a double life.

Anime

The Apothecary Diaries

Sub-genre: Historical, Mystery, Medical Drama.

Director: Norihiro Naganuma

Starring (Voices): Yusuke Kobayashi, Makoto Furukawa


Synopsis: Maomao, an apothecary's daughter, is kidnapped and sold into service within the Emperor’s palace. Using her extensive knowledge of poisons and medicine, she solves various medical mysteries and palace conspiracies, eventually catching the eye of an influential eunuch named Jinshi.


Dr. Stone

Sub-genre: Sci-Fi, Adventure, Post-apocalyptic.

Director: Shinya Iino

Starring (Voices): Yusuke Kobayashi, Makoto Furukawa


Synopsis: Several millennia after a mysterious phenomenon turns all of humanity into stone, a brilliant, science-loving boy named Senku Ishigami awakens. He vows to use his scientific knowledge to rebuild civilization from the ground up, starting a "Kingdom of Science" in a world that has returned to nature.


Spy x Family

Sub-genre: Action-Comedy, Spy, Slice-of-life.

Director: Kazuhiro Furuhashi

Starring (Voices): Takuya Eguchi, Atsumi Tanezaki, Saori Hayami


Synopsis: To complete a high-stakes mission, a master spy creates a fake family, unaware that his "wife" is a professional assassin and his "daughter" is a telepath. The three must navigate the challenges of being a "normal" family while hiding their secret identities from each other and the world.

Filipino Drama

Ang Mutya ng Section E

Sub-genre: Teen Romance, Comedy, High School.

Director: Theodore Boborol

Starring: Ashtine Olviga, Andres Muhlach


Synopsis: Jay-jay, a girl who grew up with boys, finds herself transferring to a school where she is the only girl in the notorious "Section E." She must navigate the chaotic energy of her rowdy classmates while slowly finding her place—and perhaps love—among them.


He's Into Her

Sub-genre: Rom-com, Coming-of-age, Teen Drama.

Director: Chad Vidanes

Starring: Donny Pangilinan, Belle Mariano


Synopsis: Maxpein, a spunky girl from the province, moves to Manila to live with her wealthy father and enrolls in a prestigious school. She immediately clashes with the popular school heartthrob, Deib Lohr, leading to a series of pranks and confrontations that eventually turn into a deep, complicated romance.


Avenues of the Diamond (University Series)

Sub-genre: New Adult, Romance, Drama.

Director: Theodore Boborol

Starring: Aubrey Caraan, Lance Carr


Synopsis: Samantha Vera, a model and student, finds her life taking an unexpected turn when her parents arrange for her to marry a medical student named Clyden Ramirez. The story follows their journey from a forced engagement to navigating the pressures of their respective careers and finding genuine love.

Western Shows

The Vampire Diaries

Sub-genre: Supernatural Drama, Horror, Romance.

Director: Kevin Williamson, Julie Plec

Starring: Nina Dobrev, Paul Wesley, Ian Somerhalder


Synopsis: High schooler Elena Gilbert falls in love with Stefan Salvatore, a centuries-old vampire, only to find herself in a love triangle with his dangerous brother, Damon. Her life is forever changed as she discovers the supernatural history of her town, Mystic Falls, involving witches, werewolves, and hunters.


Wednesday

Sub-genre: Supernatural Mystery, Comedy Horror, Coming-of-age.

Director: Tim Burton (Lead Director)

Starring: Jenna Ortega, Emma Myers, Hunter Doohan


Synopsis: Wednesday Addams is sent to Nevermore Academy, a boarding school for outcasts, after her latest expulsion. While there, she attempts to master her emerging psychic abilities, solve a monstrous killing spree, and uncover a mystery that involved her parents 25 years ago.


Bridgerton

Sub-genre: Regency Romance, Period Drama, Alternate History.

Director: Chris Van Dusen / Jess Brownell

Starring: Adjoa Andoh, Julie Andrews (voice), Golda Rosheuvel (Ensemble Cast)


Synopsis: Set in the competitive world of Regency Era London’s ton, the eight siblings of the powerful Bridgerton family search for love and marriage. Their lives are scrutinized by an anonymous and sharp-tongued gossip columnist known only as Lady Whistledown.
