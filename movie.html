Fantasy

Charlie and the Chocolate Factory (2005)

Sub-genre: Musical, Comedy, Surrealism.

Director: Tim Burton

Starring: Johnny Depp, Freddie Highmore, Helena Bonham Carter


Synopsis: A young boy named Charlie Bucket wins a rare golden ticket to tour the secretive and magical chocolate factory owned by the eccentric Willy Wonka. Alongside four other children, he experiences a world of candy wonders while Wonka searches for a worthy heir to his empire.


Alice in Wonderland (2010)

Sub-genre: Action-Adventure, Dark Fantasy, Gothic.

Director: Tim Burton

Starring: Mia Wasikowska, Johnny Depp, Helena Bonham Carter, Anne Hathaway


Synopsis: Nineteen-year-old Alice returns to the whimsical Underland, a place she visited as a child but has since forgotten. She must reunite with the Mad Hatter and her old friends to fulfill a prophecy by slaying the Jabberwocky and ending the Red Queen's tyrannical reign.


Nanny McPhee (2005)

Sub-genre: Family, Comedy, Period Drama.

Director: Kirk Jones

Starring: Emma Thompson, Colin Firth, Thomas Brodie-Sangster


Synopsis: A widowed father struggles to manage his seven incredibly mischievous children who have driven away every previous caregiver. Everything changes when the mysterious and magical Nanny McPhee arrives, using her enchantments to transform the children's behavior through five essential lessons.

Classic

Mary Poppins (1964)

Sub-genre: Fantasy, Family, Comedy.

Director: Robert Stevenson

Starring: Julie Andrews, Dick Van Dyke, David Tomlinson


Synopsis: When a magical nanny descends from the sky using her umbrella, she takes charge of the two neglected children of a cold London banker. Through whimsical adventures and catchy songs, she helps the family rediscover the joy of life and the importance of spending time together.


The Sound of Music (1965)

Sub-genre: Drama, Biography, Family.

Director: Robert Wise

Starring: Julie Andrews, Christopher Plummer


Synopsis: Maria, a lively young woman struggling to become a nun, is sent to serve as a governess for the seven children of a stern, widowed naval officer. She brings music and laughter back into their lives, but the family's happiness is soon threatened by the onset of World War II and the Nazi annexation of Austria.


Barbie (2023)

Sub-genre: Comedy, Fantasy, Satire, Coming-of-age.

Director: Greta Gerwig

Starring: Margot Robbie, Ryan Gosling, America Ferrera


Synopsis: After experiencing a series of strange "imperfections," Stereotypical Barbie leaves the colorful paradise of Barbieland to seek answers in the Real World. Her journey of self-discovery challenges her views on perfection, womanhood, and the complexities of human emotion.

Romance

10 Things I Hate About You (1999)

Sub-genre: Rom-com, High School Drama, Shakespearean Adaptation.

Director: Gil Junger

Starring: Julia Stiles, Heath Ledger, Joseph Gordon-Levitt


Synopsis: In this modern retelling of Shakespeare’s The Taming of the Shrew, a popular high school girl is forbidden from dating until her older, cynical sister Kat finds a boyfriend. A plan is hatched to pay the school's "bad boy" to woo Kat, leading to an unexpected and complicated romance.


50 First Dates (2004)

Sub-genre: Rom-com, Drama, Slapstick Comedy.

Director: Peter Segal

Starring: Adam Sandler, Drew Barrymore


Synopsis: Henry falls deeply in love with Lucy, only to discover that she suffers from short-term memory loss and forgets him every single night. He commits himself to winning her heart anew every single morning, creating a series of heartwarming and comedic first dates to sustain their relationship.


The Great Gatsby (2013)

Sub-genre: Period Drama, Tragedy, Literary Realism.

Director: Baz Luhrmann


Starring: Leonardo DiCaprio, Tobey Maguire, Carey Mulligan

Synopsis: Set in the Roaring Twenties, the story follows Nick Carraway as he becomes entangled in the life of his mysterious millionaire neighbor, Jay Gatsby. Gatsby’s lavish parties are all a front for his desperate obsession to reunite with Daisy Buchanan, leading to a tragic tale of wealth, love, and disillusionment.

Mystery

Enola Holmes 1 (2020)

Sub-genre: Action-Adventure, Crime, Period Mystery.

Director: Harry Bradbeer

Starring: Millie Bobby Brown, Henry Cavill, Sam Claflin


Synopsis: When Enola Holmes wakes up to find her mother missing, she embarks on a dangerous journey to London to find her. While evading her famous brother Sherlock, she becomes a formidable detective in her own right as she unravels a conspiracy surrounding a young runaway Lord.


Nancy Drew (2007)

Sub-genre: Comedy, Family, Teen Detective.

Director: Andrew Fleming

Starring: Emma Roberts, Josh Flitter, Max Thieriot


Synopsis: The teenage sleuth Nancy Drew travels to Los Angeles with her father and stumbles upon a long-unsolved mystery involving the death of a glamorous movie star. Despite warnings to stop, she uses her wits and classic detective skills to piece together clues hidden within an old mansion.


Pokémon Detective Pikachu (2019)

Sub-genre: Action-Comedy, Sci-Fi, Urban Fantasy.

Director: Rob Letterman

Starring: Ryan Reynolds (voice), Justice Smith, Kathryn Newton


Synopsis: In a city where humans and Pokémon live together, Tim Goodman teams up with a wisecracking, coffee-addicted Pikachu that only he can understand. Together, they navigate a complex mystery to find Tim’s missing father, uncovering a plot that threatens the peaceful coexistence of their entire world.

Anime

Spirited Away (2001)

Sub-genre: Fantasy, Coming-of-age, Supernatural.

Director: Hayao Miyazaki

Starring (English Dub): Daveigh Chase, Jason Marsden, Suzanne Pleshette


Synopsis: While moving to a new home, young Chihiro and her parents wander into a magical realm where her parents are transformed into pigs. To save them, she must work in a mystical bathhouse ruled by a cruel witch and navigate a world filled with strange spirits and forgotten gods.


Howl’s Moving Castle (2004)

Sub-genre: Romantic Fantasy, Anti-war, Steampunk.

Director: Hayao Miyazaki

Starring (English Dub): Emily Mortimer, Christian Bale, Billy Crystal


Synopsis: After a spiteful witch curses her with the body of an old woman, Sophie seeks out the legendary wizard Howl for help. She finds herself living in his magical walking castle, where she becomes part of an extraordinary family and works to break both her curse and Howl’s own mysterious contract.


My Neighbor Totoro (1988)

Sub-genre: Family, Slice-of-life, Magical Realism.

Director: Hayao Miyazaki

Starring: Dakota Fanning, Elle Fanning (Disney dub)


Synopsis: Two young sisters move to a rural home to be closer to their hospitalized mother and soon discover that the nearby forest is inhabited by wondrous spirits. They befriend a giant, fluffy forest protector named Totoro, who leads them on magical adventures that help them cope with their family's uncertainty.
