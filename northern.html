Northern Lights, Norway

The footsole of God's throne




Jump to Bottom


About the Place: The Northern Lights in Norway is a natural light display that paints the night sky with vivid shades of green, pink, purple, and sometimes red. Caused by charged particles from the sun colliding with Earth’s atmosphere near the magnetic poles, the auroras create dancing, ethereal patterns that seem almost magical. Norway’s northern regions, especially above the Arctic Circle, offer some of the best viewing spots due to clear skies and minimal light pollution. Tourists often combine aurora hunting with winter activities like dog sledding, snowmobiling, or staying in glass igloos for an unforgettable experience.

Why I Want To Visit: It's the Northern Lights, the footsole of Heaven. Everyone wants to visit this place, including me. I like the feeling of getting awe-struck by surreal sceneries and I believe this place will have that effect on me.

Fun Fact: The Northern Lights are caused by charged particles from the sun colliding with Earth’s atmosphere near the poles, not by sunlight reflecting off the Earth — these collisions excite atmospheric gases, which then glow in the vivid colors seen in the aurora.
